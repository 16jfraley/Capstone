Code Of Conduct

(November 2022)


Individual Responsibility


All members are at all times subject to this Code of Conduct and the associated Disciplinary Procedures.

All participants at BDGG events will abide by the Code of Conduct and be considered as members during the event regardless of paid membership status.

Persons are expected to conduct themselves in ways which display the following:
1. Good standards of behaviour during club events and in personal affairs.
2. A sporting approach to other players, opponents, officials and spectators.
3. Behaviour fitting good ambassadors for the club and the sport of disc golf.


Any person of the club who falls below these standards, thereby bringing themselves or the BDGG into disrepute shall be subjected to disciplinary action.

The club will endeavour to provide a safe and harassment free environment within its activities for all members.

Any incident occurring during our events should be reported to the club officer in charge of the event who shall stand as chairman when reporting to the management committee. All other cases should be reported to the sitting chairman.

(See Disciplinary and Appeal Process) The Management Committee shall investigate in confidence any reported harassment or unacceptable conduct.

Unacceptable Conduct


Abusive or threatening behavior

Bullying in any form

Harassment of Officers with regard to Decisions or Policies implemented by such officers or harassment of any member, is deemed to be completely unacceptable and in this context, harassment will be defined as follows: Comments or remarks which are gratuitously critical, insulting, humiliating, or intimidating, whether by using innuendo or spreading malicious gossip

These definitions are not exhaustive

the above apply to any such comments made on social media
